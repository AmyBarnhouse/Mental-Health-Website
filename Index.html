How the percentage of young people with mental health issues differs by age:

Anxiety Anxiety disorders are one of the most commonly diagnosed mental health problems among students. Anxiety is what we feel when we are worried, tense or afraid â€“ particularly about things that are about to happen, or which we think could happen in the future. Anxiety can become a mental health problem if it impacts on your ability to live your life as fully as you want to. Depression Depression is the most commonly diagnosed mental health problem among students. Depression is a low mood that lasts for a long time, and affects your everyday life.

Dealing with the Stigma

Many people remain silent when it comes to tackling mental health problems. Through speaking out and sharing experiences, others can gain the confidence to contribute.

Try to connect yourself with friends to prevent feeling isolated. There are many treatments available to you, delivered in different ways to suit preferences.

This graph illustrates the probability of children/young people having a mental disorder, based on whether or not they have sought help for mental concerns. The data has been split into age range, as well as gender. Select a viewing option to see noticeable trends.

  • The probability of disorder strongly decreases for those who don't seek help, but there is still a moderate percentage of young people left untreated.
  • Children are most likely to be correctly diagnosed from ages 11-16 years old.
  • Boys are less likely to have a mental disorder at any age, independent of recieving aid or not.

This data is made available by NHS Digital 'Mental Health of Children and Young People in England, 2021 wave 2 follow up to the 2017 survey'.
